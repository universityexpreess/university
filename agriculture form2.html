Agriculture Form 2 Topical Questions and Answers
Soil Fertility (ii):
  1. Explain the concept of soil fertility and its importance in agricultural productivity.
  2. Discuss the factors that contribute to soil fertility and how they affect crop growth.
  3. Describe the role of organic matter in maintaining soil fertility and improving soil structure.
  4. What are the common methods used to improve soil fertility, and how do they impact crop yields?
  5. Explain the importance of soil testing and nutrient management in maintaining optimal soil fertility levels.
  6. Discuss the impact of soil erosion and soil degradation on soil fertility and agricultural sustainability.
  7. How does soil pH affect nutrient availability and soil fertility, and how can it be managed?
  8. Describe the role of microorganisms such as bacteria and fungi in enhancing soil fertility.
  9. What are the benefits of crop rotation and cover cropping in maintaining soil fertility?
  10. Reflect on the importance of sustainable soil management practices for long-term soil fertility and agricultural productivity.
Crop Production (ii) - Planting:
  1. Discuss the factors to consider when selecting suitable crops for planting in a specific area.
  2. Explain the importance of soil preparation before planting and the techniques involved.
  3. Describe the various methods of planting crops, including direct seeding and transplanting.
  4. How does spacing and row arrangement impact crop growth and yield during planting?
  5. Discuss the role of planting depth and seed quality in successful crop establishment.
  6. Explain the concept of seed treatment and its benefits in promoting healthy crop growth.
  7. What are the considerations for planting time and seasonality when planning crop production?
  8. Describe the techniques used for water management during planting and early crop growth stages.
  9. Discuss the importance of weed control strategies during the planting phase of crop production.
  10. Reflect on the challenges and solutions related to planting operations in agricultural systems.
Crop Production (iii) - Nursery Practice:
  1. Define nursery practice in crop production and its significance in plant propagation.
  2. Discuss the materials and equipment needed for establishing and managing a plant nursery.
  3. Describe the techniques used for seed selection, germination, and seedling production in a nursery.
  4. How do environmental factors such as temperature, humidity, and light affect nursery plant growth?
  5. Explain the process of transplanting seedlings from the nursery to the main field.
  6. Discuss the importance of proper watering, nutrient management, and pest control in nursery management.
  7. What are the benefits of using nursery-raised seedlings compared to direct seeding in crop production?
  8. Describe the challenges faced in nursery practice and strategies for overcoming them.
  9. Discuss the economic and environmental advantages of incorporating nursery practices in crop production systems.
  10. Reflect on the role of research and innovation in improving nursery techniques for sustainable agriculture.
Crop Production (iv) - Field Practices:
  1. Explain the importance of land preparation and field layout in crop production.
  2. Discuss the techniques used for land leveling, plowing, harrowing, and furrow creation in field practices.
  3. Describe the process of seedbed preparation and its impact on crop establishment and growth.
  4. How do crop rotation and intercropping contribute to effective field management and pest control?
  5. Explain the concept of soil conservation practices and their role in sustainable field management.
  6. Discuss the importance of timely planting, weeding, and irrigation in field crop production.
  7. What are the considerations for fertilizer application, nutrient management, and soil health in field practices?
  8. Describe the techniques used for crop protection, including pest and disease management strategies.
  9. Discuss the role of mechanization and modern technologies in enhancing field practices and agricultural productivity.
  10. Reflect on the challenges and opportunities in implementing field practices for successful crop production.
Crop Production (v) - Vegetables:
  1. Discuss the importance of vegetable crops in agricultural systems and their nutritional value.
  2. Describe the factors to consider when selecting suitable vegetable crops for cultivation.
  3. Explain the techniques used for soil preparation, planting, and spacing in vegetable production.
  4. How does crop rotation and companion planting benefit vegetable crop health and yield?
  5. Discuss the irrigation requirements and water management practices for vegetable cultivation.
  6. Explain the importance of nutrient management, fertilization, and soil pH for vegetable crops.
  7. Describe common pests, diseases, and weeds affecting vegetable crops and their control measures.
  8. Discuss the techniques used for harvesting, post-harvest handling, and storage of vegetable produce.
  9. What are the economic considerations and market opportunities for vegetable farming?
  10. Reflect on the sustainable practices and innovations in vegetable production for food security and livelihood improvement.
Livestock Health (i) - Introduction:
  1. Define livestock health and its importance in animal production systems.
  2. Discuss the factors that contribute to livestock health, including genetics, nutrition, and environment.
  3. Describe the signs and symptoms of healthy livestock and indicators of potential health problems.
  4. Explain the importance of vaccination, deworming, and preventive healthcare in livestock management.
  5. Discuss the role of biosecurity measures in preventing disease outbreaks and maintaining herd health.
  6. How do environmental factors such as housing, ventilation, and sanitation impact livestock health?
  7. Describe the procedures for routine health checks, disease monitoring, and record-keeping in livestock farms.
  8. Discuss the common diseases and health issues affecting livestock and their management strategies.
  9. What are the challenges and opportunities in promoting good health practices and welfare for livestock?
  10. Reflect on the ethical considerations and responsibilities of livestock owners and caretakers in ensuring animal health and well-being.
Livestock Health (ii) - Parasites:
  1. Define parasites in the context of livestock health and discuss the types of parasites that affect farm animals.
  2. Describe the life cycles of common livestock parasites and their impact on animal health and productivity.
  3. Explain the symptoms and signs of parasitic infections in livestock and methods of diagnosis.
  4. Discuss the risks associated with internal and external parasites in livestock and their economic implications.
  5. Describe the preventive measures and control strategies for managing parasitic infestations in livestock.
  6. How do grazing management practices and pasture rotation help reduce parasite burden in livestock?
  7. Discuss the use of anthelmintic drugs and deworming protocols in parasite control programs for livestock.
  8. Explain the concept of integrated parasite management (IPM) and its application in livestock systems.
  9. What are the challenges and concerns related to parasite resistance and sustainable parasite control in livestock?
  10. Reflect on the role of research and advancements in parasitology in improving livestock health outcomes.
Livestock Production (ii) - Nutrition:
  1. Define livestock nutrition and its importance in animal growth, development, and productivity.
  2. Discuss the nutritional requirements of different livestock species, including ruminants, monogastrics, and poultry.
  3. Describe the categories of nutrients essential for livestock diets, such as carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins, and minerals.
  4. Explain the concept of balanced ration formulation and the principles of feed management for optimal livestock nutrition.
  5. Discuss the role of forages, concentrates, supplements, and additives in meeting livestock nutritional needs.
  6. How do factors such as age, breed, physiological status, and environmental conditions influence livestock nutritional requirements?
  7. Describe common nutritional disorders and deficiencies in livestock and their effects on health and performance.
  8. Discuss the impact of feeding practices, feeding schedules, and feeding systems on livestock nutrition.
  9. What are the considerations for feed quality, feed storage, and feed safety in livestock production?
  10. Reflect on sustainable feeding practices, alternative feed sources, and innovations in livestock nutrition for efficient and environmentally responsible production.
