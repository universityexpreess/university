Biology Form 1 Topical Questions and Answers
Introduction to Biology
  1. Define biology and explain its importance in understanding life.
  2. Discuss the characteristics of living organisms.
  3. Differentiate between living and non-living things.
  4. Describe the levels of organization in living organisms, from cells to ecosystems.
  5. Explain the principles of the cell theory.
  6. Discuss the differences between plant and animal cells.
  7. Describe the functions of the major organelles found in eukaryotic cells.
  8. Explain the concept of homeostasis and its significance in living organisms.
  9. Discuss the importance of adaptation in the survival of organisms.
  10. Describe the process of classification and taxonomy in biology.
Classsificstion 1
  1. Define classification and explain its importance in biology.
  2. Discuss the reasons why organisms need to be classified.
  3. Explain the hierarchical levels of classification used by biologists.
  4. Describe the contributions of Carolus Linnaeus to the field of biological classification.
  5. Discuss the criteria used in the classification of organisms.
  6. Explain the difference between artificial and natural classification systems.
  7. Describe the major kingdoms of living organisms and their distinguishing characteristics.
  8. Discuss the importance of binomial nomenclature in naming organisms.
  9. Explain the concept of evolutionary relationships and how they influence classification.
  10. Discuss the limitations of traditional classification systems in light of modern molecular techniques.
The Cell
  1. Define a cell and discuss its significance as the basic unit of life.
  2. Describe the contributions of Robert Hooke and Anton van Leeuwenhoek to the discovery of cells.
  3. Differentiate between prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells.
  4. Explain the structure and function of the cell membrane.
  5. Discuss the role of the nucleus in a eukaryotic cell.
  6. Describe the structure and function of the cytoplasm.
  7. Explain the significance of organelles such as mitochondria and chloroplasts in cellular processes.
  8. Discuss the importance of ribosomes in protein synthesis within cells.
  9. Explain the structure and function of the endoplasmic reticulum (ER) in a cell.
  10. Describe the role of the Golgi apparatus in cellular transport and secretion.
Cell Physiology
  1. Explain the process of diffusion and its importance in cellular physiology.
  2. Discuss the role of osmosis in maintaining cellular homeostasis.
  3. Describe the process of active transport and its significance in cellular functions.
  4. Explain the concept of endocytosis and give examples of its types.
  5. Discuss the importance of exocytosis in cellular physiology.
  6. Describe the structure and function of the cell membrane in regulating cell processes.
  7. Explain the significance of cellular respiration in providing energy for cell activities.
  8. Discuss the role of photosynthesis in plant cells and its importance in the ecosystem.
  9. Describe the process of cellular metabolism and its regulation within cells.
  10. Explain how enzymes facilitate biochemical reactions within cells and discuss factors affecting enzyme activity.
Nutrition In Plants and Animals
  1. Discuss the process of photosynthesis and its significance in plant nutrition.
  2. Explain how plants obtain water and minerals from the soil for their nutrition.
  3. Describe the role of chlorophyll in photosynthesis.
  4. Discuss the factors affecting the rate of photosynthesis in plants.
  5. Explain the concept of autotrophic nutrition in plants.
  6. Describe the structure and function of stomata in plant leaves.
  7. Discuss the importance of transpiration in plant nutrition and water transport.
  8. Explain the process of digestion in animals.
  9. Discuss the role of enzymes in the digestion of carbohydrates, proteins, and lipids.
  10. Describe the functions of the major components of the human digestive system.
