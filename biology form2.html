Biology Form 2 Topical Questions and Answers
Transport in Plants
  1. What are the two main types of vascular tissues in plants involved in transport? Explain their functions.
  2. Describe the process of transpiration and its role in water movement within plants.
  3. How are minerals absorbed by plant roots and transported to other parts of the plant?
  4. Explain the significance of the xylem and phloem in the transport of water, nutrients, and sugars in plants.
  5. What is the cohesion-tension theory, and how does it explain water movement in plants?
  6. Discuss the role of stomata in regulating gas exchange and water loss in plants during photosynthesis.
  7. How does the structure of plant roots facilitate the absorption and transport of water and nutrients?
  8. Describe the process of phloem loading and unloading in plants and its importance in sugar transport.
  9. What are the factors that influence the rate of transpiration in plants?
  10. Explain how plants adapt to different environmental conditions to optimize water and nutrient uptake.
Transport in Animals (Circulatory System):
  1. What are the main components of the circulatory system in animals, and what are their functions?
  2. Compare and contrast the structure and function of arteries, veins, and capillaries in the circulatory system.
  3. Describe the double circulation system in mammals and its advantages in maintaining efficient blood flow.
  4. What is the role of red blood cells and hemoglobin in oxygen transport within the circulatory system?
  5. Explain the process of blood clotting and its importance in wound healing and preventing excessive bleeding.
  6. Discuss the factors that regulate blood pressure in the human body.
  7. How does the lymphatic system contribute to immune function and fluid balance in the body?
  8. Describe the structure and function of the heart chambers, valves, and blood vessels in the circulatory system.
  9. What are the differences between systemic circulation and pulmonary circulation?
  10. Explain the relationship between the respiratory system and the circulatory system in oxygen delivery and carbon dioxide removal.
Respiration in Plants and Animals:
  1. Compare and contrast aerobic and anaerobic respiration in terms of energy production and end products.
  2. Describe the process of cellular respiration in plants, including glycolysis, the Krebs cycle, and oxidative phosphorylation.
  3. What are the respiratory substrates used by plants and animals, and how are they converted into energy?
  4. Explain the role of mitochondria in cellular respiration and ATP production in both plants and animals.
  5. How do plants exchange gases during respiration, and what are the adaptations for efficient gas exchange?
  6. Discuss the differences in respiration between plants and animals in terms of respiratory organs and mechanisms.
  7. What is the significance of the electron transport chain in cellular respiration?
  8. Describe the role of enzymes in facilitating biochemical reactions during respiration in living organisms.
  9. Explain how oxygen and carbon dioxide are transported in the blood and tissues of animals.
  10. Discuss the effects of exercise and physical activity on respiratory rate and oxygen consumption in animals.
Gaseous Exchange in Plants:
  1. Describe the process of gaseous exchange in plants during photosynthesis and respiration.
  2. What are stomata, and how do they facilitate gaseous exchange in plant leaves?
  3. Explain the role of guard cells in regulating the opening and closing of stomata.
  4. Discuss the adaptations of plants in arid environments for efficient gaseous exchange while minimizing water loss.
  5. How do aquatic plants perform gaseous exchange, and what are the challenges they face compared to terrestrial plants?
  6. Describe the structure and function of the leaf mesophyll cells in facilitating gaseous exchange.
  7. Explain how environmental factors such as light intensity, temperature, and humidity affect gaseous exchange in plants.
  8. What is the significance of the concentration gradient in driving the movement of gases during plant respiration and photosynthesis?
  9. Discuss the role of diffusion and osmosis in gaseous exchange processes within plant cells.
  10. How do plants transport gases such as oxygen and carbon dioxide between different plant organs?
Gaseous Exchange in Animals:
  1. Describe the structure and function of the respiratory system in animals for gaseous exchange.
  2. What are the differences between the respiratory organs of aquatic and terrestrial animals?
  3. Explain the process of breathing in animals, including inspiration and expiration.
  4. How do fish and other aquatic animals obtain oxygen from water, and what adaptations do they have for gaseous exchange?
  5. Discuss the role of respiratory pigments such as hemoglobin and hemocyanin in oxygen transport in animals.
  6. What is the Bohr effect, and how does it affect oxygen release in tissues during respiration?
  7. Explain how respiratory surfaces such as alveoli in mammals and tracheal systems in insects facilitate efficient gaseous exchange.
  8. Discuss the mechanisms of gas exchange in amphibians, including cutaneous respiration and lung respiration.
  9. How does the respiratory system in birds differ from that of mammals, and what advantages does it offer for efficient oxygen uptake?
  10. Describe the effects of environmental factors such as altitude and pollution on animal respiratory physiology.
Excretion and Homeostasis:
  1. What is excretion, and why is it essential for maintaining homeostasis in living organisms?
  2. Describe the structure and function of the human excretory system, including the kidneys, ureters, bladder, and urethra.
  3. Explain the processes of filtration, reabsorption, and secretion in the nephron during urine formation.
  4. Discuss the role of hormones such as ADH (antidiuretic hormone) and aldosterone in regulating water and electrolyte balance.
  5. How do the kidneys regulate blood pH and excrete metabolic wastes such as urea and uric acid?
  6. Describe the excretory systems of other vertebrates such as fish, amphibians, reptiles, and birds, highlighting adaptations to different environments.
  7. What are the functions of the liver in detoxification, metabolism, and excretion of waste products?
  8. Explain the process of sweat production and its role in thermoregulation and waste removal in humans.
  9. Discuss the effects of kidney diseases such as nephritis and kidney stones on excretory function and overall health.
  10. How do plants and unicellular organisms excrete wastes, and what mechanisms do they use to maintain internal balance and homeostasis?
