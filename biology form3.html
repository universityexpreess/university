Biology Form 3 Topical Questions and Answers
Classification II:
  1. What is taxonomy, and why is it important in the study of living organisms?
  2. Describe the hierarchical levels of classification used in taxonomy, from domain to species.
  3. Explain the binomial nomenclature system developed by Carl Linnaeus for naming species.
  4. Discuss the criteria used in modern classification systems to group organisms into kingdoms and phyla.
  5. What are the major differences between prokaryotic and eukaryotic organisms, and how are they classified?
  6. Describe the characteristics of the five kingdoms of living organisms and provide examples of organisms in each kingdom.
  7. Discuss the concept of evolutionary relationships and phylogenetic trees in biological classification.
  8. Explain the role of cladistics and molecular techniques in modern taxonomy and phylogenetic analysis.
  9. What are the advantages and limitations of using DNA sequencing data in classifying organisms?
  10. How do taxonomists classify organisms that do not fit neatly into traditional classification schemes, such as viruses or hybrid species?
Ecology:
  1. Define ecology and describe its scope in studying interactions between organisms and their environment.
  2. Discuss the levels of ecological organization, from individuals to ecosystems, and their interrelationships.
  3. Explain the concept of an ecosystem and describe the biotic and abiotic components that make up an ecosystem.
  4. What are trophic levels, and how do they illustrate energy flow in food chains and food webs?
  5. Describe the different types of ecological pyramids (energy pyramid, biomass pyramid, and pyramid of numbers) and their significance.
  6. Discuss the factors that influence population growth and dynamics, including birth rates, death rates, immigration, and emigration.
  7. Explain the concept of carrying capacity and its role in regulating population sizes within ecosystems.
  8. Describe the major terrestrial and aquatic biomes and their characteristic features, biodiversity, and adaptations.
  9. What are the primary ecological roles of producers, consumers, and decomposers in nutrient cycling and ecosystem functioning?
  10. Discuss the impact of human activities such as deforestation, pollution, and climate change on ecosystems and biodiversity.
Reproduction in Plants and Animals:
  1. Compare and contrast asexual and sexual reproduction in terms of advantages, disadvantages, and examples from plants and animals.
  2. Describe the structure and function of the male and female reproductive organs in flowering plants.
  3. Explain the process of pollination in plants, including self-pollination and cross-pollination mechanisms.
  4. What are the differences between monocotyledonous and dicotyledonous seeds, and how do they relate to plant reproduction?
  5. Discuss the stages of embryonic development in plants, from fertilization to seed formation.
  6. Describe the different types of seed dispersal mechanisms and their ecological significance.
  7. Explain the menstrual cycle in humans, including the hormonal changes, ovulation, and preparation for fertilization.
  8. Discuss the reproductive strategies of animals, including oviparous, viviparous, and ovoviviparous species.
  9. What are the functions of male and female reproductive systems in animals, and how do they contribute to fertilization and offspring development?
  10. Describe the process of sexual reproduction in flowering plants, from pollination to seed germination and plant growth.
Growth and Development:
  1. Define growth and development in biological terms and explain the differences between the two processes.
  2. Describe the cellular processes involved in growth, including cell division (mitosis and meiosis) and cell differentiation.
  3. What are hormones, and how do they regulate growth and development in plants and animals?
  4. Explain the stages of human growth and development from infancy to adulthood, including physical, cognitive, and emotional changes.
  5. Discuss the factors that influence growth rates and patterns in plants and animals, such as genetics, nutrition, and environmental conditions.
  6. Describe the life cycle of a flowering plant, including germination, vegetative growth, flowering, pollination, and seed formation.
  7. What are the differences between primary growth and secondary growth in plant tissues, and how do they contribute to plant development?
  8. Explain the concept of metamorphosis in insects and other invertebrates and how it relates to their growth and life cycles.
  9. Discuss the role of environmental cues and stimuli in triggering developmental processes in organisms.
  10. Describe the concept of senescence and aging in plants and animals, including the effects of aging on physiological functions and lifespan.
