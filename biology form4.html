Biology Form 4 Topical Questions and Answers
Genetics:
  1. Define genetics and explain its significance in understanding inheritance and variation in organisms.
  2. Discuss the contributions of Gregor Mendel to the field of genetics, including his experiments and principles of inheritance.
  3. Describe the structure and function of DNA, including the roles of genes, alleles, and chromosomes in genetic information.
  4. Explain the difference between genotype and phenotype, and how they relate to an organism's characteristics.
  5. Discuss the principles of Mendelian genetics, including dominance, segregation, and independent assortment.
  6. What is a Punnett square, and how is it used to predict the outcomes of genetic crosses?
  7. Describe the different types of genetic mutations, including point mutations, insertions, deletions, and chromosomal mutations.
  8. Explain the concepts of codominance, incomplete dominance, and multiple alleles in genetic inheritance.
  9. Discuss the role of environmental factors and gene-environment interactions in shaping phenotypic traits.
  10. How do biotechnology techniques such as genetic engineering and gene editing contribute to advancements in genetics and biomedicine?
Evolution:
  1. Define evolution and explain the principles of natural selection proposed by Charles Darwin.
  2. Describe the evidence for evolution, including fossil records, comparative anatomy, embryology, and molecular biology.
  3. Explain the concept of adaptation and how it relates to the survival and reproductive success of organisms in their environments.
  4. Discuss the mechanisms of evolution, including genetic drift, gene flow, mutation, and natural selection.
  5. What is speciation, and how does it occur through allopatric and sympatric mechanisms?
  6. Describe the role of mutations and genetic variation in driving evolutionary change within populations.
  7. Explain the concept of convergent evolution and provide examples from different taxa.
  8. Discuss the evolutionary relationships between species and the construction of phylogenetic trees.
  9. How does the study of homologous and analogous structures support the theory of evolution?
  10. What are the major patterns of evolution, including divergent evolution, coevolution, and adaptive radiation?
Reception, Response, and Coordination in Plants and Animals:
  1. Describe the structure and function of plant hormones and their roles in growth, development, and responses to environmental stimuli.
  2. Explain the process of photoperiodism in plants and how it influences flowering and other physiological responses.
  3. Discuss the mechanisms of plant tropisms, including phototropism, geotropism, and thigmotropism.
  4. What is a plant's response to stress factors such as drought, salinity, and temperature extremes?
  5. Describe the structure and function of the nervous system in animals, including neurons, synapses, and neurotransmitters.
  6. Explain how nerve impulses are generated, transmitted, and coordinated in the nervous system.
  7. Discuss the roles of sensory receptors, such as photoreceptors, chemoreceptors, and mechanoreceptors, in detecting stimuli in animals.
  8. Describe the reflex arc and its role in producing rapid responses to stimuli in animals.
  9. What are the differences between the central nervous system (CNS) and the peripheral nervous system (PNS) in animals?
  10. How do animals coordinate responses to internal and external stimuli through hormonal regulation and neural pathways?
Support and Movements in Plants and Animals:
  1. Describe the structure and function of plant tissues involved in support and mechanical strength, including collenchyma, sclerenchyma, and xylem.
  2. Explain the role of turgor pressure and cell wall rigidity in maintaining plant cell shape and providing support.
  3. Discuss the adaptations of plants for support and anchorage in different environments, such as aerial roots, buttress roots, and tendrils.
  4. What is transpiration pull, and how does it contribute to water transport and support in plants?
  5. Describe the structure and function of the skeletal system in animals, including bones, cartilage, and joints.
  6. Explain the types of animal movements, including voluntary and involuntary movements, locomotion, and behavior.
  7. Discuss the role of muscles, tendons, and ligaments in animal movement and coordination.
  8. What are the differences between endoskeletons and exoskeletons in animals, and how do they provide support?
  9. Explain how animals maintain balance and posture through sensory feedback and coordination of muscular actions.
  10. Describe the mechanisms of locomotion in animals, including walking, running, flying, swimming, and crawling.
