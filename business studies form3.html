Business Studies Form2 Topical Questions and Answers
Demand and Supply
  1. Define demand and supply in the context of economics, and explain their relationship in determining market equilibrium.
  2. Describe the factors that influence demand for goods and services in Kenya, including price, income, consumer preferences, and population changes.
  3. How does the law of demand apply to businesses in Kenya, and what are the exceptions to this law?
  4. Discuss the concept of price elasticity of demand and its importance in understanding consumer responsiveness to price changes in Kenya.
  5. Explain the concept of supply and the factors that affect the supply of goods and services in the Kenyan market, such as production costs, technology, and government policies.
  6. How do businesses in Kenya respond to changes in demand and supply conditions, such as through pricing strategies, production adjustments, and inventory management?
  7. Discuss the role of government interventions, such as subsidies, taxes, and price controls, in influencing demand and supply dynamics in Kenya.
  8. What is market equilibrium, and how is it determined by the intersection of demand and supply curves in the Kenyan market?
  9. Analyze the impact of external factors, such as global events, natural disasters, and technological advancements, on demand and supply trends in Kenya.
  10. How can businesses use demand and supply analysis to make informed decisions regarding production levels, pricing strategies, and market expansion in Kenya?
Size and Location of Firm:
  1. Define the concept of firm size and explain the factors that determine whether a business is classified as small, medium, or large in Kenya.
  2. How does the size of a firm impact its operations, management structure, market reach, and access to resources in the Kenyan business environment?
  3. Discuss the advantages and disadvantages of operating a small-scale business compared to a large-scale enterprise in Kenya.
  4. Explain the importance of location for businesses and how factors such as proximity to customers, suppliers, transportation networks, and infrastructure influence location decisions in Kenya.
  5. How do businesses in Kenya assess and choose the optimal location for setting up new facilities or branches, considering both urban and rural areas?
Product Markets:
  1. Define product markets and differentiate between perfect competition, monopoly, monopolistic competition, and oligopoly in the Kenyan market context.
  2. Discuss the characteristics of perfect competition and monopoly markets, including the number of sellers, product differentiation, price determination, and barriers to entry.
  3. How do businesses in Kenya differentiate their products and use branding, packaging, and marketing strategies to gain a competitive edge in the market?
  4. Explain the concept of market segmentation and target markets, and how businesses in Kenya identify and cater to specific customer segments.
  5. Analyze the impact of government regulations, consumer preferences, technological advancements, and globalization on product markets in Kenya.
Chain of Distribution:
  1. Define the chain of distribution and explain its components, including producers, wholesalers, retailers, and consumers, in the context of business operations in Kenya.
  2. Describe the functions of wholesalers and retailers in the distribution process, including bulk breaking, storage, transportation, and selling to end consumers.
  3. How do businesses in Kenya select and manage distribution channels to ensure efficient and effective delivery of goods and services to customers?
  4. Discuss the role of logistics and supply chain management in optimizing the distribution process for businesses operating in Kenya.
  5. What are the challenges and opportunities faced by businesses in managing their distribution networks, including inventory control, warehousing, and last-mile delivery?
National Income:
  1. Define national income and explain the methods used to calculate national income in Kenya, such as the income approach, expenditure approach, and output approach.
  2. Discuss the components of national income, including wages, salaries, profits, rent, interest, and government transfers, and their contribution to the economy.
  3. How does national income measurement help policymakers, businesses, and economists analyze economic performance, income distribution, and standard of living in Kenya?
  4. Analyze the factors that influence changes in national income, such as economic growth, inflation, unemployment, government spending, and international trade.
  5. Explain the concept of gross domestic product (GDP), gross national income (GNI), and per capita income, and their significance in evaluating the economic health of Kenya.
Population and Employment
  1. Describe the demographic characteristics of the population in Kenya, including age distribution, urban-rural migration, education levels, and workforce participation rates.
  2. Discuss the relationship between population growth, labor supply, and employment opportunities in Kenya, including the impact on industries and sectors.
  3. How do businesses in Kenya address workforce diversity, talent management, and human resource development to meet their staffing needs and productivity goals?
  4. Explain the concept of unemployment and its types (such as frictional, structural, and cyclical) in the Kenyan labor market, including causes and consequences.
  5. Analyze the role of government policies, education and training programs, and entrepreneurship initiatives in promoting employment generation and economic empowerment in Kenya.
Net Worth of a Business:
  1. Define net worth and explain how it is calculated for a business entity in Kenya, including the components of assets, liabilities, and owner's equity.
  2. Discuss the significance of net worth as a measure of financial health, solvency, and wealth accumulation for businesses in Kenya.
  3. How do businesses manage their net worth through financial planning, investment decisions, debt management, and capital structure strategies?
  4. Explain the difference between book value and market value in assessing the net worth of a business, and their implications for valuation and financial analysis.
  5. Analyze the factors that can affect changes in a business's net worth over time, such as profitability, asset appreciation, debt repayment, and economic conditions.
Business Transactions:
  1. Define business transactions and classify them into revenue transactions, expense transactions, capital transactions, and non-cash transactions in the context of accounting for businesses in Kenya.
  2. Describe the double-entry system of accounting and how it is used to record and analyze business transactions accurately and systematically.
  3. How do businesses in Kenya maintain transaction records using journals, ledgers, and accounting software, and why is record-keeping important for financial management?
  4. Explain the concept of accrual accounting versus cash accounting, and their implications for recognizing revenue, expenses, and financial reporting in Kenya.
  5. Discuss the role of internal controls, audits, and financial statements in ensuring the accuracy, reliability, and transparency of business transactions in Kenya.
The Ledger
  1. Define the ledger and explain its role in recording and summarizing financial transactions for businesses in Kenya.
  2. Discuss the different types of ledgers used in accounting, including the general ledger, subsidiary ledgers, and control accounts, and how they are organized.
  3. How are ledger accounts classified based on the accounting equation (Assets = Liabilities + Equity), and how do businesses post transactions to ledger accounts?
  4. Explain the process of balancing ledger accounts, including the trial balance, adjusting entries, and closing entries, to prepare accurate financial statements.
  5. Analyze the importance of maintaining a well-organized and up-to-date ledger system for financial analysis, decision-making, and regulatory compliance in Kenya.
The Cashbook:
  1. Define the cashbook and explain its purpose in recording cash transactions for businesses in Kenya, including cash receipts, cash payments, and bank transactions.
  2. Discuss the differences between a single-column cashbook, double-column cashbook, and three-column cashbook, and their respective uses in cash management.
  3. How do businesses reconcile bank statements with cashbook records to ensure accuracy and detect discrepancies in cash balances in Kenya?
  4. Explain the concept of petty cash and how it is managed using a petty cashbook for small expenditures and day-to-day cash transactions in businesses.
  5. Analyze the importance of cash flow management, cash budgeting, and liquidity monitoring for businesses in Kenya, especially in managing working capital and financial stability.
