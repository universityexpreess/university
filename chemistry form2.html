Chemistry Form 2 Topical Questions and Answers
Structure of the Atom:
  1. What are the subatomic particles that make up an atom, and what are their respective charges and locations within the atom?
  2. Describe the structure of an atom according to the Bohr's atomic model and the modern quantum mechanical model.
  3. Explain the concept of atomic number and mass number, and how they are used to identify elements.
  4. Discuss the significance of isotopes in understanding atomic structure and their practical applications.
  5. How are electron configurations represented using electron shells, subshells, and orbitals?
  6. Describe the trends in atomic size, ionization energy, and electron affinity across a period and down a group in the periodic table.
  7. What is the difference between an ion and an isotope, and how do they affect chemical behavior?
  8. Explain the process of electron configuration and orbital filling using the Aufbau principle, Pauli exclusion principle, and Hund's rule.
  9. Discuss the contributions of scientists such as J.J. Thomson, Ernest Rutherford, and Niels Bohr to our understanding of atomic structure.
  10. How does the arrangement of electrons in atoms influence chemical properties and reactivity?
Chemical Families:
  1. Define a chemical family and provide examples of families in the periodic table, such as alkali metals, halogens, and noble gases.
  2. Describe the general properties and trends within each chemical family, including atomic size, ionization energy, and reactivity.
  3. Explain the characteristics of alkali metals, including their low melting points, high reactivity with water, and tendency to form ionic compounds.
  4. Discuss the properties of halogens, such as their diatomic nature, electronegativity, and reactivity with metals.
  5. What are the noble gases, and why are they generally unreactive and stable?
  6. Compare and contrast the properties of metals, nonmetals, and metalloids based on their positions in the periodic table.
  7. Explain the trends in chemical reactivity within a group and across a period in the periodic table.
  8. Discuss the properties and uses of transition metals, including their variable oxidation states, catalytic activity, and magnetic properties.
  9. Describe the characteristics of metalloids, including their intermediate properties between metals and nonmetals.
  10. How do the properties of elements within a chemical family contribute to their applications in industry, technology, and everyday life?
Structure and Bonding:
  1. Define a chemical bond and explain the difference between ionic, covalent, and metallic bonds.
  2. Describe the formation of ionic bonds through the transfer of electrons between atoms, using examples such as sodium chloride.
  3. Explain the concept of electronegativity and its role in determining bond polarity and ionic character.
  4. Discuss the characteristics of covalent bonds, including shared electron pairs, bond lengths, and bond energies.
  5. How are Lewis structures and electron dot diagrams used to represent covalent bonds and molecular structures?
  6. Describe the properties of molecular compounds, such as low melting points, poor conductivity, and solubility in nonpolar solvents.
  7. Explain the concept of resonance structures and how they contribute to the stability of certain molecules.
  8. Discuss the VSEPR theory and its application in predicting molecular geometries and bond angles.
  9. What are coordinate covalent bonds, and how do they differ from typical covalent bonds?
  10. How does molecular polarity affect the physical properties and behavior of substances, such as solubility, melting points, and intermolecular forces?
Carbon and Its Compounds:
  1. Describe the unique properties of carbon that allow it to form a wide variety of compounds and structures.
  2. Explain the concept of organic compounds and hydrocarbons, including alkanes, alkenes, alkynes, and aromatic compounds.
  3. Discuss the structural isomerism and stereoisomerism observed in organic compounds and their significance.
  4. What are functional groups, and how do they determine the chemical properties and reactivity of organic molecules?
  5. Describe the properties and uses of alcohols, ethers, aldehydes, ketones, carboxylic acids, and esters.
  6. Explain the differences between saturated and unsaturated hydrocarbons, including their molecular structures and properties.
  7. Discuss the nomenclature rules for naming organic compounds, including IUPAC naming conventions.
  8. What are polymers, and how are they formed through polymerization reactions? Provide examples of natural and synthetic polymers.
  9. Describe the properties and uses of hydrocarbons in industry, energy production, and everyday products.
  10. How do environmental issues such as pollution, greenhouse gas emissions, and climate change relate to the chemistry of carbon compounds?
