  2. Discuss the significance of the Sabbath day in the creation narrative. Why is it considered a sacred day in Judeo-Christian tradition?

  8. Analyze the themes of sin, redemption, and forgiveness in the narratives of Adam and Eve, Noah, and the Tower of Babel. How do these stories contribute to the overall message of salvation in Christian theology?

  10. Discuss the relevance of the creation and fall narratives in Genesis to contemporary ethical and moral issues. How can Christians apply the lessons learned from these stories in their daily lives?
Faith and God Promises to Abraham

  7. Describe the challenges and obstacles faced by Abraham and Sarah in their journey of faith. How do they overcome these challenges with God's help?
  8. Reflect on the theological themes of faith, obedience, and divine providence in the narratives of Abraham and Sarah. How do these themes continue to inspire believers today?
  9. Discuss the relevance of God's promises to Abraham for understanding the concept of salvation in Christian theology. How does the fulfillment of these promises point to God's redemptive plan for humanity?
  10. Reflect on your own journey of faith in light of the stories of Abraham and Sarah. What lessons can you learn from their example of trust and reliance on God's promises?

  5. Discuss the ethical and moral principles outlined in the Ten Commandments, which form the core of the Sinai Covenant. How do these commandments guide the behavior and conduct of believers?

  7. Reflect on the consequences of breaking the covenant and disobeying God's commandments as outlined in the book of Exodus. How do these consequences shape the narrative of Israelite history?
  8. Discuss the enduring legacy of the Sinai Covenant in Jewish and Christian theology. How does it continue to influence religious practices and beliefs today?
  9. Analyze the concept of covenantal relationship between God and humanity in the context of the Sinai Covenant. What does it reveal about God's faithfulness and the human response to His promises?
  10. Reflect on the relevance of the Sinai Covenant for understanding the relationship between faith and obedience in contemporary religious contexts. How can believers uphold the principles of the covenant in their daily lives?

Loyalty to God

  4. Explain the role of miracles and signs in Elijah's ministry, such as the drought, the widow of Zarephath, and the raising of the widow's son. How do these miracles demonstrate God's power and Elijah's faithfulness?
  5. Discuss Elijah's experience in the cave at Mount Horeb (Sinai) as narrated in 1 Kings 19. What lessons can be learned from God's encounter with Elijah during his time of discouragement and despair?
  6. Describe Elijah's mentorship of Elisha as his successor. How does their relationship exemplify the passing of the prophetic mantle from one generation to the next?
  7. Reflect on Elijah's role as a voice of conscience and righteousness in the face of political corruption and idolatry in Israel. How does his example inspire believers to stand firm in their loyalty to God?
  8. Discuss the symbolism of Elijah's ascension to heaven in a chariot of fire. What does this event signify in terms of Elijah's faithfulness and God's recognition of his service?
  9. Explore the lessons learned from Elijah's life and ministry in terms of fidelity to God amidst opposition and persecution. How can his example inspire Christians to remain steadfast in their faith in challenging times?
  10. Reflect on the relevance of Elijah's message and mission for contemporary believers. How can his story encourage Christians to prioritize loyalty to God above all other allegiances and interests?
