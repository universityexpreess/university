Christian Religious Education form2 Topical Questions and Answers
Old Testament Prophecies about the Messiah:
  1. According to Luke's Gospel, which Old Testament prophet foretold that the Messiah would be born of a virgin? (Isaiah 7:14; Luke 1:26-35)
  2. How does Luke connect the birth of Jesus in Bethlehem to the prophecy mentioned in Micah 5:2?
  3. Discuss the significance of the genealogy of Jesus presented in Luke 3:23-38 in relation to the Old Testament prophecies about the Messiah.
  4. What Old Testament prophecies did John the Baptist fulfill as the forerunner of the Messiah? (Isaiah 40:3; Malachi 3:1; Luke 1:76-79)
  5. How did the people in Jesus' time expect the Messiah to fulfill the prophecies regarding the restoration of Israel's kingdom?
  6. Describe the angel Gabriel's announcement to Mary regarding the birth of the Messiah and its fulfillment in Jesus' life.
  7. Explain how the events surrounding Jesus' birth in Bethlehem fulfilled specific Old Testament prophecies.
  8. How does Luke's Gospel emphasize Jesus' role as the fulfillment of the Messianic prophecies throughout his ministry?
  9. Discuss the Messianic prophecies related to Jesus' suffering and crucifixion as depicted in Luke's Gospel.
  10. How did Jesus himself reference and fulfill the Old Testament prophecies about the Messiah during his earthly ministry according to Luke?
The Infancy and Early Life of Jesus:
  1. Describe the events of the Annunciation to Mary as recorded in Luke's Gospel and its significance for Jesus' early life.
  2. How does Luke's Gospel portray the birth of Jesus in Bethlehem, including the angelic announcement to the shepherds?
  3. Explain the significance of Jesus' presentation at the temple according to the Law of Moses as mentioned in Luke 2:22-38.
  4. Discuss Jesus' early years in Nazareth and how they are described in Luke's Gospel.
  5. What insights does Luke's Gospel provide about Jesus' childhood and upbringing in relation to his divine identity?
  6. How did Jesus' family respond to his early signs of wisdom and knowledge in the temple? (Luke 2:41-52)
  7. Discuss the role of John the Baptist as the herald of Jesus' ministry and his connection to Jesus' early life.
  8. Explain the concept of the "hidden years" of Jesus' life in Nazareth and their significance in understanding his ministry.
  9. What does Luke's Gospel reveal about Jesus' relationship with his parents and his growth in wisdom and favor with God and man?
  10. How do the early events in Jesus' life as narrated in Luke's Gospel lay the foundation for his public ministry and messianic identity?
The Galilean Ministry:
  1. Discuss the significance of Jesus' baptism by John the Baptist and the heavenly affirmation recorded in Luke 3:21-22.
  2. Describe Jesus' temptations in the wilderness as recorded in Luke's Gospel and their significance for his ministry.
  3. Identify at least three miracles performed by Jesus during his Galilean ministry as recorded in the Gospel of Luke.
  4. How did Jesus call his first disciples according to Luke's Gospel, and what message did he give them? (Luke 5:1-11)
  5. Explain the purpose and significance of Jesus' teaching in parables during his Galilean ministry.
  6. Discuss Jesus' authority over nature and his power to perform miracles as seen in Luke's Gospel.
  7. How did Jesus' interactions with various individuals and groups in Galilee demonstrate his compassion and message of salvation?
  8. Describe Jesus' teachings on faith, forgiveness, and kingdom principles as depicted in Luke's Gospel during his Galilean ministry.
  9. What impact did Jesus' miracles and teachings have on the people of Galilee, and how did they respond to his ministry?
  10. Discuss the challenges and opposition faced by Jesus during his Galilean ministry as narrated in Luke's Gospel and how he overcame them.
The Journey to Jerusalem (Luke 9-18):
  1. Describe the significance of Jesus' transfiguration on the mountain as recorded in Luke's Gospel. (Luke 9:28-36)
  2. Explain the lessons learned from Jesus' teachings on humility and servanthood during the journey to Jerusalem. (Luke 9:46-48; 22:24-27)
  3. Discuss the parable of the Good Samaritan and its message about compassion and neighborly love. (Luke 10:25-37)
  4. How did Jesus' interactions with Martha and Mary illustrate the importance of spiritual priorities? (Luke 10:38-42)
  5. Describe the teachings of Jesus on prayer and the model prayer he gave to his disciples. (Luke 11:1-13)
  6. Explain the significance of Jesus' teachings on discipleship, including taking up one's cross and following him. (Luke 9:23-27; 14:25-33)
  7. Discuss the lessons learned from Jesus' encounters with Zacchaeus and the rich ruler regarding wealth and salvation. (Luke 19:1-10; 18:18-30)
  8. How did Jesus' predictions of his suffering and death during the journey to Jerusalem prepare his disciples for the events to come? (Luke 18:31-34)
  9. Describe the triumphal entry of Jesus into Jerusalem and the significance of Palm Sunday. (Luke 19:28-44)
  10. What were some of the challenges and opposition faced by Jesus during his journey to Jerusalem, and how did he respond to them according to Luke's Gospel?
The Jerusalem Ministry:
  1. Explain the significance of Jesus' cleansing of the temple in Jerusalem and its message about true worship. (Luke 19:45-48)
  2. Discuss the teachings of Jesus in the temple during his Jerusalem ministry, including the parables and confrontations with religious leaders. (Luke 20-21)
  3. Describe Jesus' Last Supper with his disciples and the institution of the Lord's Supper. (Luke 22:7-23)
  4. How did Jesus demonstrate humility and servanthood through his actions, such as washing the disciples' feet? (Luke 22:24-30; John 13:1-17)
  5. Discuss the events of Jesus' betrayal, arrest, and trial in Jerusalem, including Peter's denial. (Luke 22-23)
  6. Explain the significance of Jesus' crucifixion and death on the cross, including the words he spoke during his crucifixion. (Luke 23:26-49)
  7. Describe the reactions of various individuals and groups to Jesus' crucifixion and death according to Luke's Gospel.
  8. What lessons can be learned from Jesus' interactions with the criminals crucified alongside him and the centurion at the cross? (Luke 23:39-43, 47)
  9. Discuss the events surrounding Jesus' burial and the reactions of his disciples and followers after his death. (Luke 23:50-56; 24:1-12)
  10. Explain the significance of Jesus' resurrection from the dead and his appearances to his disciples in Jerusalem. (Luke 24)
The Passion, Death, and Resurrection of Jesus:
  1. Describe the events leading up to Jesus' arrest in the Garden of Gethsemane and his prayer during that time. (Luke 22:39-46)
  2. Explain the significance of Judas' betrayal of Jesus and the role it played in the events leading to Jesus' crucifixion. (Luke 22:47-53)
  3. Discuss the trials of Jesus before the Jewish leaders, Pilate, and Herod, highlighting the accusations and verdicts against him. (Luke 22:66-23:25)
  4. How did Pilate and Herod respond to Jesus during his trials, and what insights did their actions reveal about Jesus' innocence? (Luke 23:13-25)
  5. Describe the journey of Jesus to Golgotha, the crucifixion, and the interactions with the criminals crucified alongside him. (Luke 23:26-43)
  6. Explain the significance of the words spoken by Jesus from the cross, including his prayers for forgiveness and his surrender to God's will. (Luke 23:34, 43, 46)
  7. Discuss the reactions of various individuals and groups to Jesus' crucifixion, including the soldiers, onlookers, and disciples. (Luke 23:44-49)
  8. How did the centurion and those present at the crucifixion respond to the signs and events surrounding Jesus' death? (Luke 23:47-48)
  9. Describe the events surrounding Jesus' burial, including Joseph of Arimathea's role and the sealing of the tomb. (Luke 23:50-56)
  10. Explain the significance of Jesus' resurrection from the dead on the third day, his appearances to the disciples, and the commissioning of the disciples for their ministry. (Luke 24:1-49)
