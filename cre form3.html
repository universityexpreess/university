Christian Religious Education form3 Topical Questions and Answers
Gifts of the Holy Spirit:
  1. What are the seven gifts of the Holy Spirit according to Christian tradition, and how are they described in the Bible?
  2. Explain the difference between the gifts of the Holy Spirit and the fruits of the Holy Spirit.
  3. Discuss the importance of the gifts of the Holy Spirit in the life and mission of the Church.
  4. How can individuals discern and cultivate the gifts of the Holy Spirit in their lives for spiritual growth and service?
  5. Describe a biblical example where one of the gifts of the Holy Spirit was manifested and its impact on the community.
  6. Discuss the role of the Holy Spirit in empowering believers to exercise the gifts for the common good.
  7. How do the gifts of the Holy Spirit contribute to the building of unity and diversity within the Body of Christ?
  8. Explain how the gifts of the Holy Spirit relate to the ministries and responsibilities of Christians in the Church.
  9. Discuss the concept of spiritual gifts assessments and their usefulness in identifying and utilizing spiritual gifts.
  10. Share personal insights or testimonies about experiencing and using the gifts of the Holy Spirit in your life or in the life of others.
The Unity of Believers:
  1. What does the Bible teach about the unity of believers and its importance in the Christian faith?
  2. Discuss the challenges and obstacles to maintaining unity among believers and how they can be addressed.
  3. Describe the characteristics of genuine Christian unity as exemplified in the early Church.
  4. How does Jesus' prayer for unity among believers in John 17 inspire and guide Christians in pursuing unity today?
  5. Explain the concept of ecumenism and its significance in fostering unity among different Christian denominations.
  6. Discuss the role of love, forgiveness, and reconciliation in promoting unity within the Church.
  7. How can diversity within the Church contribute positively to unity without compromising core beliefs and values?
  8. Share examples of initiatives or activities that promote unity among believers in your community or church.
  9. Discuss the biblical principles and teachings that guide Christians in resolving conflicts and disagreements within the Church.
  10. Reflect on the benefits and blessings of experiencing unity among believers and its impact on witness and ministry.
Teachings from Selected Old Testament:
  1. Summarize the key teachings and themes from the book of Psalms in the Old Testament.
  2. Discuss the significance of the Ten Commandments as foundational teachings for ethical living in the Old Testament.
  3. Explain the concept of covenant and its importance in understanding God's relationship with humanity in the Old Testament.
  4. Describe the wisdom literature in the Old Testament (e.g., Proverbs, Ecclesiastes) and its teachings on practical living.
  5. Discuss the prophetic messages and teachings of Isaiah regarding justice, mercy, and the coming Messiah.
  6. How do the narratives and teachings in the book of Genesis provide foundational truths about creation, sin, and redemption?
  7. Explain the teachings of the book of Job regarding suffering, faith, and divine sovereignty.
  8. Discuss the relevance of the historical narratives in the Old Testament (e.g., Exodus, Judges) for contemporary Christian living.
  9. How did the Old Testament teachings and prophecies prepare the way for the coming of Jesus Christ?
  10. Reflect on a specific Old Testament teaching or story that has personally impacted your faith journey and explain its significance.
Prophet Amos:
  1. Discuss the historical and cultural context in which the prophet Amos lived and delivered his message.
  2. Summarize the key themes and messages of the book of Amos regarding social justice, moral integrity, and the consequences of sin.
  3. How did Amos challenge the religious leaders and people of Israel regarding their hypocrisy and lack of genuine worship?
  4. Describe the visions and symbolic actions used by Amos to convey God's message to the people.
  5. Discuss the relevance of Amos' prophetic message for addressing social and ethical issues in contemporary society.
  6. Explain the role of prophets like Amos in calling individuals and nations to repentance and righteousness.
  7. How did Amos' prophecies about judgment and restoration foreshadow God's ultimate plan for redemption through Jesus Christ?
  8. Share examples from the book of Amos that illustrate God's concern for the marginalized and oppressed in society.
  9. Discuss the response of the people of Israel to Amos' prophetic warnings and the lessons that can be learned from their reactions.
  10. Reflect on the implications of Amos' teachings for Christians today in terms of social justice, compassion, and faithfulness to God.
Prophet Jeremiah:
  1. Describe the background and calling of the prophet Jeremiah as recorded in the Bible.
  2. Summarize the key themes and messages of the book of Jeremiah regarding repentance, judgment, and hope.
  3. How did Jeremiah confront the religious leaders and people of Judah regarding their idolatry and disobedience to God?
  4. Discuss the metaphorical symbols and actions used by Jeremiah to convey God's prophetic message to the nation.
  5. Explain the significance of Jeremiah's prophecies about the new covenant and the restoration of God's people.
  6. How did Jeremiah's personal experiences and struggles as a prophet influence his message and ministry?
  7. Describe the challenges and opposition faced by Jeremiah in proclaiming God's word, including his imprisonment and persecution.
  8. Discuss the role of hope and restoration in Jeremiah's prophecies despite the impending judgment on Judah.
  9. How does the book of Jeremiah address themes of faithfulness, perseverance, and trust in God during times of adversity?
  10. Reflect on the lessons and insights that Christians can glean from the life and ministry of the prophet Jeremiah for navigating challenging times and remaining faithful to God's call.
Nehemiah:
  1. Describe the historical background and context of Nehemiah's leadership and rebuilding efforts in Jerusalem.
  2. Summarize the key events and challenges faced by Nehemiah in rebuilding the walls of Jerusalem.
  3. How did Nehemiah demonstrate leadership qualities such as vision, determination, and reliance on prayer?
  4. Discuss the spiritual revival and reforms initiated by Nehemiah among the people of Judah.
  5. Explain the significance of Nehemiah's prayer and confession on behalf of the nation regarding their sins and the covenant.
  6. Describe Nehemiah's efforts to address social injustices and economic disparities among the returned exiles.
  7. How did Nehemiah handle opposition and external threats while carrying out the rebuilding project?
  8. Discuss the importance of teamwork and collaboration in achieving Nehemiah's goals for the restoration of Jerusalem.
  9. How did Nehemiah's leadership exemplify qualities of humility, integrity, and devotion to God?
  10. Reflect on the lessons and principles of leadership, faithfulness, and community engagement found in the book of Nehemiah and their relevance for Christians today.
