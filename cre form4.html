Christian Religious Education form4 Topical Questions and Answers
Introduction to Christian Ethics:
  1. Define Christian ethics and explain its foundational principles as derived from the teachings of Jesus Christ.
  2. Discuss the role of Scripture, tradition, reason, and experience in shaping Christian ethical decision-making.
  3. How do Christian ethics address moral dilemmas and ethical challenges in contemporary society?
  4. Explain the concept of moral relativism and contrast it with the Christian perspective on objective moral truths.
  5. Discuss the ethical considerations related to issues such as abortion, euthanasia, and genetic engineering from a Christian standpoint.
  6. Describe the importance of virtues such as love, honesty, compassion, and justice in Christian ethical living.
  7. How does the concept of the common good guide Christian ethics in promoting social justice and community well-being?
  8. Discuss the ethical responsibilities of Christians in areas such as environmental stewardship, social justice, and human rights advocacy.
  9. How can Christian ethics contribute to ethical leadership and decision-making in various spheres of life, including politics and business?
  10. Reflect on a specific ethical dilemma or situation and apply Christian ethical principles to analyze and address it.
Christian Approaches to Human Sexuality, Marriage, and Family:
  1. Discuss the biblical view of human sexuality and the sanctity of marriage as taught in Christian ethics.
  2. Describe the Christian understanding of gender roles, sexual ethics, and the value of purity and chastity.
  3. How do Christian teachings on marriage emphasize commitment, fidelity, and mutual respect between spouses?
  4. Explain the importance of family values, parenting, and nurturing relationships within the context of Christian ethics.
  5. Discuss the ethical considerations related to issues such as divorce, remarriage, and blended families from a Christian perspective.
  6. Describe the Christian teachings on sexual intimacy, contraception, and responsible parenthood.
  7. How does the concept of the family as a domestic church influence Christian approaches to marriage and family life?
  8. Discuss the role of forgiveness, reconciliation, and healing in addressing challenges within marriages and families.
  9. How can Christian communities support and strengthen marriages and families through pastoral care, counseling, and education?
  10. Reflect on the biblical examples of healthy relationships and family dynamics and their relevance for Christian families today.
Christian Approaches to Work:
  1. Define the Christian concept of work and its significance in fulfilling God's purposes and glorifying Him.
  2. Discuss the biblical teachings on diligence, honesty, integrity, and stewardship in the context of Christian work ethics.
  3. How does the Christian worldview shape attitudes toward vocation, career choices, and the dignity of labor?
  4. Explain the Christian perspective on wealth creation, economic justice, and fair labor practices.
  5. Discuss the ethical considerations related to workplace relationships, leadership responsibilities, and conflict resolution in Christian ethics.
  6. Describe the biblical teachings on Sabbath rest, work-life balance, and the importance of restorative practices.
  7. How can Christians integrate faith and work, living out their beliefs and values in professional settings?
  8. Discuss the ethical implications of globalization, technology, and environmental sustainability in the context of Christian approaches to work.
  9. How do Christian principles guide decision-making regarding business practices, entrepreneurship, and social responsibility?
  10. Reflect on personal experiences or observations related to work and ethics, applying Christian principles to evaluate and improve work practices.
Christian Approaches to Leisure:
  1. Define leisure and discuss the Christian perspective on the purpose and value of leisure activities.
  2. How do Christian ethics guide choices and behaviors in leisure pursuits, entertainment, and recreational activities?
  3. Explain the concept of Sabbath rest and its significance in balancing work, leisure, and spiritual renewal.
  4. Discuss the ethical considerations related to leisure activities, including issues such as materialism, addiction, and unhealthy lifestyles.
  5. Describe the Christian teachings on moderation, self-control, and responsible use of leisure time and resources.
  6. How can Christians engage in leisure activities that promote personal growth, well-being, and positive community interactions?
  7. Discuss the role of leisure in fostering relationships, building community, and strengthening family bonds from a Christian perspective.
  8. Explain the biblical principles regarding stewardship of time, talents, and resources in leisure pursuits.
  9. How can Christian communities provide wholesome and enriching leisure opportunities for individuals and families?
  10. Reflect on the balance between leisure, rest, and spiritual renewal in your own life, applying Christian values and principles.
Christian Approaches to Wealth, Money, and Poverty:
  1. Discuss the Christian perspective on wealth, possessions, and material resources as outlined in the Bible.
  2. Describe the biblical teachings on generosity, stewardship, and the responsibilities of wealth from a Christian ethical standpoint.
  3. How does the concept of economic justice inform Christian approaches to wealth distribution, poverty alleviation, and social welfare?
  4. Explain the ethical considerations related to issues such as greed, consumerism, and inequalities of wealth and opportunity in Christian ethics.
  5. Discuss the teachings of Jesus and the apostles regarding wealth, poverty, and the Kingdom of God.
  6. Describe the Christian response to poverty and social injustice, including strategies for empowerment, advocacy, and community development.
  7. How can Christians engage in charitable giving, philanthropy, and compassionate outreach as expressions of Christian stewardship?
  8. Discuss the role of Christian organizations, ministries, and churches in addressing issues of poverty, homelessness, and economic marginalization.
  9. Explain the biblical principles regarding debt, financial responsibility, and ethical business practices in Christian ethics.
  10. Reflect on personal attitudes, behaviors, and choices related to wealth, money management, and generosity, applying Christian values and teachings.
Approaches to Law, Order, and Justice:
  1. Define the Christian perspective on law, justice, and the role of government as outlined in the Bible.
  2. Discuss the biblical teachings on righteousness, fairness, and the pursuit of justice in society from a Christian ethical standpoint.
  3. How does the concept of divine law and moral absolutes inform Christian approaches to legal systems and governance?
  4. Explain the ethical considerations related to issues such as crime, punishment, and rehabilitation in Christian ethics.
  5. Discuss the teachings of Jesus and the apostles regarding love for neighbor, forgiveness, and reconciliation in matters of justice.
  6. Describe the role of Christians in promoting social justice, human rights, and equality under the law.
  7. How can Christian ethics guide attitudes and actions toward law enforcement, judicial systems, and respect for authority?
  8. Discuss the biblical principles regarding accountability, integrity, and transparency in governance and public service.
  9. Explain the Christian response to societal issues such as corruption, oppression, and discrimination within legal frameworks.
  10. Reflect on personal experiences or observations related to law, order, and justice, applying Christian values and teachings to evaluate and improve justice systems
