English Topical Questions and Answers
Listening and Speaking:
  1. Describe a memorable experience you had recently and share it with your classmates.
  2. Listen to a short audio clip about the importance of recycling and discuss its key points with a partner.
  3. Give a two-minute oral presentation on a topic of your choice, using visual aids if possible.
Pronunciation:
  1. Practice pronouncing the following words: "comfortable," "schedule," "vegetable."
  2. Identify and correct the pronunciation errors in the following sentences: "I am goink to the beech next weekend."
  3. Explain the importance of clear pronunciation in effective communication.
English Sounds: Vowels and Consonant Sounds:
  1. List five vowel sounds in English and provide an example word for each.
  2. Identify the consonant sounds in the word "elephant" and categorize them as voiced or voiceless.
  3. Describe the differences between long and short vowel sounds in English.
Listening Comprehension and Note Taking:
  1. Listen to a short passage about a famous historical figure and take notes on their achievements and contributions.
  2. Summarize a TED Talk or podcast episode you listened to recently, highlighting the main points.
  3. Discuss with a partner the strategies you use for effective note-taking during listening activities.
Listening and Responding to Oral Narratives:
  1. Listen to an excerpt from a short story or novel and discuss the plot, characters, and themes with your classmates.
  2. Role-play a scene from a play or novel with a partner, focusing on dialogue and character interaction.
  3. Reflect on how listening to oral narratives enhances your understanding and appreciation of literature.
Mastery of Content:
  1. Describe the main themes and messages conveyed in a poem or short story you recently studied.
  2. Discuss the characters and their motivations in a novel or play you have read.
  3. Explain the significance of literary devices such as symbolism or irony in a piece of literature.
Use of Courteous Language:
  1. Role-play a scenario where you need to politely decline an invitation.
  2. Write a thank-you note to a teacher or classmate expressing appreciation for their help or support.
  3. Discuss the importance of using courteous language in different social contexts.
Parts of Speech:
  1. Identify the parts of speech (nouns, verbs, adjectives, etc.) in the sentence: "The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog."
  2. Create a sentence using each of the eight parts of speech.
  3. Discuss the role of each part of speech in constructing meaningful sentences.
Phrases:
  1. Identify and classify the following phrases: "in the park," "under the table," "with a smile."
  2. Use each of the following phrases in a sentence: "at the beach," "on the bus," "from the mountain."
  3. Explain the function of phrases in adding detail and complexity to sentences.
Reading Skills:
  1. Read a short passage and identify the main idea and supporting details.
  2. Practice skimming and scanning by quickly locating specific information in a text.
  3. Discuss the strategies you use for improving reading comprehension and retention.
Fairy Tales, Poems, Plays, and Short Stories:
  1. Summarize the plot of a fairy tale or short story you recently read, focusing on key events and characters.
  2. Analyze the themes and symbolism in a poem you studied in class.
  3. Perform a dramatic reading of a scene from a play or act out a short story with your classmates.
