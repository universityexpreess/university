Mathematics form 1 Topical Questions and Answers
Natural numbers
  1. What are natural numbers? Provide three examples.
  2. Write the first five natural numbers in ascending order.
  3. Explain the concept of successor and predecessor of natural numbers.
Factors
  1. Define factors of a number and give three factors of 12.
  2. Find the factors of 20 and list them.
  3. Determine whether 15 is a factor of 75.
Divisibility Test
  1. State the divisibility rule for 2, 3, 4, 5, and 10.
  2. Apply the divisibility test to determine if 63 is divisible by 3.
  3. Use the divisibility rule to check if 120 is divisible by 4.
Greatest Common Divisor:
  1. Define the greatest common divisor (GCD) of two numbers.
  2. Find the GCD of 18 and 24 using the prime factorization method.
  3. Determine the GCD of 36 and 48 using the Euclidean algorithm.
Least Common Multiple:
  1. Define the least common multiple (LCM) of two numbers.
  2. Find the LCM of 6 and 8 using the prime factorization method.
  3. Determine the LCM of 9 and 12 using the list method.
Integers:
  1. Define integers and give examples of positive and negative integers.
  2. Find the opposite of -7.
  3. Add -5 and 3 to find their sum.
Fractions:
  1. Define fractions and give an example of a proper fraction.
  2. Simplify the fraction 8/12 to its lowest terms.
  3. Add 2/5 and 3/10, then express the result in its simplest form.
Decimals:
  1. Define decimals and give an example of a terminating decimal.
  2. Convert 0.75 into a fraction in its simplest form.
  3. Add 0.6 and 0.35 to find their sum.
Squares and Square Roots:
  1. Define squares and square roots of numbers.
  2. Calculate the square root of 81.
  3. Determine the square of 7.
Algebraic Expressions:
  1. Define algebraic expressions and provide an example.
  2. Simplify the expression 3x + 2y - x - y.
  3. Evaluate the expression 4a - 2b for a = 3 and b = 5.
Rate, Ratio, Proportion, and Percentage:
  1. Calculate the ratio of boys to girls in a class of 30 students if there are 18 boys.
  2. If a car travels 240 kilometers in 4 hours, what is its speed in kilometers per hour?
  3. If 30% of students in a school are girls, and there are 450 boys, how many students are there in total?
  4. Express 3 hours as a percentage of a day.
  5. A recipe calls for 2 cups of flour and 1 cup of sugar. What is the ratio of flour to sugar?
Length:
  1. Convert 2.5 meters to centimeters.
  2. If the length of a rectangle is 12 cm and its width is 5 cm, what is its perimeter?
  3. How many millimeters are there in 1.5 meters?
  4. A piece of string measures 45 cm. If it is cut into three equal pieces, what is the length of each piece?
  5. The distance between two towns is 180 kilometers. If a car travels at an average speed of 60 km/h, how long will the journey take?
Area:
  1. Calculate the area of a rectangle with length 8 cm and width 5 cm.
  2. Find the area of a triangle with base 6 cm and height 9 cm.
  3. If the area of a square is 49 square meters, what is the length of each side?
  4. Determine the area of a circle with radius 4 cm. Use π = 3.14.
  5. A garden has a length of 12 meters and a width of 8 meters. What is the area of the garden?
Volume and Capacity:
  1. Calculate the volume of a cube with side length 5 cm.
  2. Find the volume of a rectangular prism with length 6 cm, width 4 cm, and height 3 cm.
  3. If a container can hold 2.5 liters of water, how many milliliters is this?
  4. Determine the volume of a cylinder with radius 3 cm and height 8 cm. Use π = 3.14.
  5. A fish tank measures 60 cm by 30 cm by 40 cm. What is its capacity in liters?
Mass, Weight, and Density:
  1. Convert 500 grams to kilograms.
  2. If the weight of an object is 12 newtons and its mass is 2 kilograms, what is the acceleration due to gravity?
  3. Calculate the density of an object with a mass of 50 grams and a volume of 25 cubic centimeters.
  4. A bag of sugar weighs 2.5 kilograms. How many grams is this?
  5. The mass of a liquid is 300 grams, and its volume is 500 cubic centimeters. What is its density?
Time:
  1. If it is 3:45 PM now, what time will it be in 2 hours and 30 minutes?
  2. Convert 2 hours and 15 minutes into minutes.
  3. If a train leaves the station at 9:20 AM and arrives at its destination at 2:45 PM, how long is the journey?
Linear Equations:
  1. Solve the equation 2x + 5 = 17.
  2. If 3y - 7 = 20, find the value of y.
  3. Solve the equation 4(2x - 3) = 20.
  4. If 5x + 8 = 33, find the value of x.
  5. Solve the equation 2(3y + 4) = 18.
Commercial Arithmetic:
  1. If the price of a book is $25 and there is a 10% discount, what is the discounted price?
  2. A shopkeeper bought 50 pens at $0.50 each and sold them at $1.00 each. What was his profit?
  3. If a DVD player costs $120 and its price increases by 25%, what is the new price?
  4. A company produces 500 units of a product and sells them at $10 each. What is their total revenue?
  5. If the cost price of an item is $80 and the selling price is $100, what is the profit percentage?
Scale Drawing:
  1. On a scale drawing, 1 cm represents 4 meters. If the length of a room on the drawing is 8 cm, what is its actual length?
  2. If a map has a scale of 1:5000, what distance does 4 cm represent on the map?
  3. On a scale drawing, 1 inch represents 10 feet. If the length of a road is 3.5 inches on the drawing, what is its actual length?
Common Solids:
  1. Calculate the surface area of a cube with side length 6 cm.
  2. Find the volume of a sphere with radius 5 cm. Use π = 3.14.
  3. Determine the lateral surface area of a cylinder with radius 4 cm and height 10 cm. Use π = 3.14.
  4. Calculate the volume of a cone with radius 3 cm and height 8 cm. Use π = 3.14
  5. sFind the surface area of a rectangular prism with length 12 cm, width 8 cm, and height 5 cm.
