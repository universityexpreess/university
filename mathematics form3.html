Mathematics form 3 Topical Questions and Answers
Quadratic Expressions and Equations:
  1. Solve the quadratic equation x2+5x−6=0 using factorization.
  2. Find the roots of the quadratic equation3x2−4x+1=0 using the quadratic formula.
  3. Determine the value(s) of k such that the quadratic equation 2x2+kx−3=0 has equal roots.
  4. Solve the inequality x2−4x>5 algebraically and represent the solution set on a number line.
  5. If p(x)=x2+6x+9, express p(x) as a product of linear factors.
Approximation of Errors:
  1. A measurement is given as 18.5 ± 0.3 Calculate the absolute and relative errors.
  2. Given that Δx=0.02 and Δy=0.03, find the absolute and relative errors for z=2x2+3y2
  3. The radius of a sphere is given as 5.2 cm with a possible error of 0.1 cm. Determine the maximum error in calculating the volume of the sphere.
  4. A student measures the length of a rectangle as 20 cm with a possible error of 0.5 cm. Find the maximum percentage error in the area of the rectangle.
Trigonometry (ii):
  1. If tanθ= 3/4 and θ is an acute angle, find the values of sinθ and cosθ.
  2. Solve for x in the equation 2cos(x+30∘)=3
  3. Determine the range of values of x in the interval 0 ≤x≤360∘ for which sinx+cosx>1.
  4. If sinα= 3/5 and α is obtuse, find the values of cosα and tanα.
