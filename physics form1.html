Force
  1. Define force and explain its importance in physics.
  2. Discuss the difference between contact and non-contact forces, providing examples of each.
  3. Explain the concept of balanced and unbalanced forces and their effects on an object.
  4. Discuss the difference between mass and weight, and how they relate to force.
  5. Describe the effects of force on the motion of objects, including acceleration, deceleration, and changes in direction.
  6. Explain the relationship between force, mass, and acceleration using Newton's second law of motion.
  7. Discuss the concept of friction and its role in opposing motion.
  8. Describe the factors that affect the force of friction between surfaces.
  9. Explain the concept of air resistance and its effect on falling objects.
  10. Discuss the role of tension in forces acting on objects connected by strings or ropes.
Pressure
  1. Define pressure and explain its significance in physics.
  2. Describe the factors that determine pressure in a fluid.
  3. Explain how pressure is distributed in a fluid at rest.
  4. Discuss the concept of atmospheric pressure and its variation with altitude.
  5. Explain the effect of depth on pressure in a fluid, using the concept of hydrostatic pressure.
  6. Discuss the relationship between pressure, force, and area using the equation for pressure.
  7. Describe the applications of Pascal's principle in hydraulic systems.
  8. Explain how pressure is used in the operation of hydraulic brakes and lifts.
  9. Discuss the concept of buoyancy and its relationship to pressure.
  10. Explain Archimedes' principle and its applications in determining the buoyant force on an object submerged in a fluid.
