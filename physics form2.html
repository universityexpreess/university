Physics Form 2 Topical Questions and Answers
Magnetism:
  1. Define magnetism and explain the difference between natural and artificial magnets.
  2. Discuss the properties of magnetic materials and their applications.
  3. Explain the concept of magnetic poles and magnetic field lines.
  4. What is magnetic induction, and how does it relate to electromagnetic induction?
  5. Describe the factors affecting the strength of a magnetic field around a magnet.
  6. Discuss the principles of electromagnetism and its practical uses.
  7. How can you demagnetize a magnet, and what factors influence its remanence?
measurements
  1. Define measurement and explain the importance of standard units in physics.
  2. Discuss the different types of measuring instruments and their uses.
  3. Explain the concept of accuracy and precision in measurements.
  4. What are the common sources of errors in measurements, and how can they be minimized?
  5. Describe the procedure for measuring length, mass, and time using appropriate instruments.
  6. Discuss the significance of scientific notation and significant figures in measurement.
  7. How can you convert between different units of measurement (e.g., metric to imperial)?
Turning Effect of Force:
  1. Define the turning effect of force and explain the concept of torque.
  2. Discuss the conditions necessary for an object to be in rotational equilibrium.
  3. Explain the principle of moments and how it applies to balancing objects.
  4. What factors affect the turning effect produced by a force?
  5. Describe the role of a pivot point (fulcrum) in determining the turning effect.
  6. Discuss the applications of the turning effect of force in everyday life.
  7. How can you increase or decrease the turning effect of a force on an object?
Equilibrium and Center of Gravity:
  1. Define equilibrium and explain the conditions for static equilibrium.
  2. Discuss the concept of center of gravity and its significance in stability.
  3. Explain how the position of the center of gravity affects the stability of an object.
  4. What factors determine the stability of an object in equilibrium?
  5. Describe the methods for finding the center of gravity of regular and irregular objects.
  6. Discuss the applications of equilibrium principles in engineering and architecture.
  7. How does the distribution of mass affect the center of gravity of an object?
Reflection at Curved Surfaces (Concave and Convex):
  1. Define reflection and explain the laws of reflection for curved surfaces.
  2. Discuss the differences between concave and convex mirrors in terms of focal length and image formation.
  3. Explain how to locate images formed by concave and convex mirrors using ray diagrams.
  4. What are the characteristics of images formed by concave and convex mirrors (real, virtual, magnified, diminished)?
  5. Describe the applications of concave and convex mirrors in optical devices.
  6. Discuss the factors that affect the size and nature of images formed by curved mirrors.
  7. How can you use mirrors to form images of objects at different distances?
Magnetic Effect of an Electric Current:
  1. Define the magnetic effect of an electric current and describe the properties of magnetic fields produced by currents.
  2. Discuss the right-hand rule and its application in determining the direction of magnetic fields around current-carrying conductors.
  3. Explain Ampere's law and its significance in understanding magnetic fields due to currents.
  4. What is the difference between solenoids and electromagnets, and how are they used?
  5. Describe the factors that affect the strength of a magnetic field produced by a current.
  6. Discuss the applications of electromagnetism in electromechanical devices.
  7. How can you increase or decrease the strength of an electromagnet?
Hooke's Law:
  1. Define Hooke's Law and explain its relationship between force and extension in a spring.
  2. Discuss the elastic limit and plastic deformation in materials.
  3. Explain how to calculate the spring constant (k) using Hooke's Law.
  4. What factors affect the behavior of a spring under applied forces?
  5. Describe the applications of Hooke's Law in springs and elastic materials.
  6. Discuss the limitations of Hooke's Law in describing material behavior.
  7. How can you experimentally verify Hooke's Law and determine the spring constant?
Waves (i):
  1. Define waves and differentiate between transverse and longitudinal waves.
  2. Discuss the properties of waves, including wavelength, frequency, amplitude, and wave speed.
  3. Explain the concepts of wave interference, reflection, and refraction.
  4. What is the principle of superposition, and how does it apply to wave behavior?
  5. Describe the behavior of waves when they encounter boundaries and obstacles.
  6. Discuss the applications of waves in various fields such as telecommunications and medicine.
  7. How can you calculate the frequency, wavelength, or wave speed of a wave given relevant information?
Sound:
  1. Define sound waves and explain how they propagate through different mediums.
  2. Discuss the properties of sound waves, including frequency, wavelength, amplitude, and speed.
  3. Explain the concepts of pitch, loudness, and quality of sound.
  4. What factors affect the speed of sound in a medium?
  5. Describe the behavior of sound waves during reflection, refraction, and diffraction.
  6. Discuss the applications of sound waves in music, communication, and medical diagnostics.
  7. How can you calculate the speed of sound in air or other materials?
Fluid Flow
  1. Define fluid flow and differentiate between laminar and turbulent flow.
  2. Discuss the factors that affect the viscosity and density of fluids.
  3. Explain the concepts of streamline flow and Bernoulli's principle.
  4. What is the relationship between pressure, velocity, and height in fluid flow?
  5. Describe the behavior of fluids in pipes and channels, including flow rate and pressure.
  6. Discuss the applications of fluid dynamics in engineering and hydraulics.
  7. How can you calculate the flow rate or pressure drop in a fluid system?
