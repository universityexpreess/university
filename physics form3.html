Physics Form 3 Topical Questions and Answers
Linear Motion:
  1. Define linear motion and describe the characteristics of objects in linear motion.
  2. Explain the difference between distance and displacement in linear motion.
  3. Discuss the concept of speed, velocity, and acceleration in linear motion.
  4. What is the difference between uniform motion and uniformly accelerated motion?
  5. Describe the equations of motion for uniformly accelerated linear motion.
  6. Discuss the factors that affect the motion of objects in a straight line.
  7. How can you graphically represent motion using distance-time and velocity-time graphs?
Refraction of Light:
  1. Define refraction of light and explain Snell's Law governing the behavior of light at boundaries.
  2. Discuss the difference between the refractive index and the speed of light in different mediums.
  3. Explain how light bends when passing from one medium to another with different refractive indices.
  4. What is the critical angle, and how is it related to total internal reflection?
  5. Describe the applications of refraction in lenses, prisms, and optical devices.
  6. Discuss the factors that affect the amount of refraction experienced by light.
  7. How can you experimentally determine the refractive index of a transparent material?
Newton's Law of Motion:
  1. State Newton's three laws of motion and explain each with examples.
  2. Discuss the concept of inertia and its relationship to Newton's first law.
  3. Explain how force, mass, and acceleration are related according to Newton's second law.
  4. What is the action-reaction pair of forces as described by Newton's third law?
  5. Describe the factors that affect the magnitude and direction of forces in a system.
  6. Discuss the applications of Newton's laws in understanding motion and designing mechanical systems.
  7. How can you calculate the net force acting on an object and its resulting acceleration?
Work, Energy, Power, and Machines:
  1. Define work, energy, and power in the context of physics.
  2. Discuss the difference between kinetic energy and potential energy.
  3. Explain the concept of mechanical advantage and efficiency in machines.
  4. What is the relationship between work, energy, and power?
  5. Describe the different types of machines and their functions.
  6. Discuss the conservation of energy and its applications in various systems.
  7. How can you calculate the work done, energy transferred, and power output in mechanical systems?
Current Electricity (ii):
  1. Define electric current and explain the factors that affect the flow of current in a circuit.
  2. Discuss Ohm's Law and its relationship between voltage, current, and resistance.
  3. Explain the concept of electrical power and how it relates to current and voltage.
  4. What are series and parallel circuits, and how do they affect current flow?
  5. Describe the behavior of resistors, capacitors, and inductors in electrical circuits.
  6. Discuss the applications of current electricity in electrical appliances and systems.
  7. How can you calculate the total resistance, current, and voltage in a circuit?
Waves (ii):
  1. Define waves and classify them into transverse and longitudinal waves.
  2. Discuss the properties of waves, including wavelength, frequency, amplitude, and wave speed.
  3. Explain the concepts of wave interference, reflection, refraction, and diffraction.
  4. What is the principle of superposition, and how does it apply to wave behavior?
  5. Describe the behavior of waves in different mediums and their transmission.
  6. Discuss the applications of waves in various fields such as sound, light, and seismic waves.
  7. How can you calculate the frequency, wavelength, wave speed, or period of a wave?
Electrostatics (ii):
  1. Define electrostatics and explain the concepts of electric charge, field, and potential.
  2. Discuss Coulomb's Law and its application in determining the force between charged objects.
  3. Explain the behavior of charged particles in electric fields and the concept of electric potential energy.
  4. What is capacitance, and how does it relate to stored electrical energy in capacitors?
  5. Describe the behavior of conductors and insulators in the presence of electric fields.
  6. Discuss the applications of electrostatics in technologies such as electrostatic precipitators and photocopying machines.
  7. How can you calculate the electric field strength, electric potential, or capacitance in a system?
Heating Effect of Electric Current:
  1. Define the heating effect of electric current and explain how it occurs in conductors.
  2. Discuss the relationship between electrical resistance, current, voltage, and power in heating elements.
  3. Explain the concept of specific heat capacity and its role in heat transfer.
  4. What factors affect the amount of heat produced by an electric current in a resistor?
  5. Describe the applications of the heating effect of electric current in appliances and devices.
  6. Discuss the safety considerations and precautions when working with electric heaters.
  7. How can you calculate the amount of heat produced or energy consumed by an electric circuit?
Quantity of Heat:
  1. Define heat as a form of energy transfer and explain the units of heat measurement.
  2. Discuss the difference between heat capacity and specific heat capacity of materials.
  3. Explain the concept of latent heat and its significance in phase changes.
  4. What is the relationship between temperature change, mass, and heat transfer?
  5. Describe the methods for measuring heat transfer, including calorimetry.
  6. Discuss the applications of heat transfer principles in thermodynamics and engineering.
  7. How can you calculate the amount of heat transferred or energy required for a process?
Gas Laws:
  1. Define the gas laws (Boyle's Law, Charles's Law, and Gay-Lussac's Law) and explain their relationships between pressure, volume, temperature, and quantity of gas.
  2. Discuss the ideal gas law equation and its application in solving gas law problems.
  3. Explain how changes in temperature and pressure affect the behavior of gases.
  4. What are the differences between ideal gases and real gases?
  5. Describe the concept of partial pressure in gas mixtures.
  6. Discuss the applications of gas laws in gas storage, compression, and expansion.
  7. How can you experimentally verify the gas laws using laboratory setups and measurements?
